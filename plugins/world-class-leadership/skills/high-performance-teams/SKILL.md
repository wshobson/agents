# High-Performance Teams

## When to Use This Skill

- Building new teams from scratch
- Improving existing team performance
- Diagnosing team dysfunction
- Scaling teams while maintaining quality
- Transitioning teams through growth stages

## Google's Project Aristotle: Five Keys to Team Success

### 1. Psychological Safety (Most Important)
**Definition**: Team members feel safe to take risks and be vulnerable

**Characteristics**:
- Can ask questions without feeling ignorant
- Can admit mistakes without fear of punishment
- Can propose ideas without ridicule
- Can challenge status quo respectfully
- Trust teammates have their back

**How to Build**:
- Leader models vulnerability (admit mistakes, ask for help)
- Frame work as learning problem, not execution problem
- Acknowledge own fallibility
- Actively invite participation and input
- Respond positively to risk-taking and questions
- No blame culture - focus on systems

### 2. Dependability
**Definition**: Team members reliably complete quality work on time

**Characteristics**:
- Follow through on commitments
- Deliver high-quality work consistently
- Meet deadlines and expectations
- Can count on each other
- Ownership and accountability

**How to Build**:
- Clear expectations and commitments
- Visible tracking of tasks and deadlines
- Address reliability issues early
- Recognize and reinforce dependable behavior
- Ensure manageable workloads
- Remove blockers proactively

### 3. Structure & Clarity
**Definition**: Clear roles, plans, and goals

**Characteristics**:
- Everyone knows their role and responsibilities
- Clear decision-making processes
- Well-defined goals and success metrics
- Understood work processes
- Explicit team norms and expectations

**How to Build**:
- RACI matrices for role clarity
- Written team charter with norms
- Clear OKRs and metrics
- Documented processes and workflows
- Regular goal review and alignment
- Explicit decision-making frameworks

### 4. Meaning
**Definition**: Work is personally important to team members

**Characteristics**:
- Work aligns with personal values
- Sense of purpose in daily work
- Connection to larger mission
- Personal growth and learning
- Using strengths and skills

**How to Build**:
- Understand individual motivations
- Connect work to company mission
- Provide variety and challenge
- Enable autonomy and ownership
- Support career development
- Align work with personal interests

### 5. Impact
**Definition**: Team members believe their work matters

**Characteristics**:
- See how work affects customers/users
- Understand business impact
- Visible progress and results
- Recognition of contributions
- Know work makes difference

**How to Build**:
- Share customer feedback and stories
- Quantify impact with metrics
- Make progress visible
- Celebrate wins and milestones
- Connect daily work to outcomes
- Bring customer voices into team

## Team Formation Best Practices

### Size: Amazon's Two-Pizza Rule
- **6-10 people maximum** per team
- Small enough for everyone to contribute
- Large enough for diverse skills
- Can be fed with two pizzas
- Smaller teams = better communication

### Composition
- **Diversity of Thought**: Different backgrounds, perspectives, experiences
- **Complementary Skills**: Cover all needed capabilities
- **Balance**: Mix of senior and junior, specialists and generalists
- **Culture Add**: Hire those who add to culture, not just fit
- **Shared Values**: Aligned on core principles

### Roles & Responsibilities
- **Clear Ownership**: Who owns what
- **RACI Matrix**: Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed
- **Avoid Ambiguity**: No overlapping unclear roles
- **Document**: Write down and share widely

## Team Development Stages (Tuckman's Model)

### Stage 1: Forming
**Characteristics**: Polite, uncertain, testing boundaries

**Leader Actions**:
- Provide clear direction and structure
- Establish purpose and goals
- Define roles and responsibilities
- Create psychological safety
- Build initial relationships

### Stage 2: Storming
**Characteristics**: Conflict, frustration, competing ideas

**Leader Actions**:
- Facilitate healthy conflict resolution
- Establish team norms together
- Address tensions directly
- Reinforce shared purpose
- Coach through disagreements

### Stage 3: Norming
**Characteristics**: Cohesion, cooperation, shared practices

**Leader Actions**:
- Reinforce positive behaviors
- Codify working agreements
- Enable autonomy
- Step back on direction
- Focus on development

### Stage 4: Performing
**Characteristics**: High performance, flow, self-management

**Leader Actions**:
- Remove blockers
- Provide resources
- Stretch with challenges
- Recognize achievements
- Protect from disruption

### Stage 5: Adjourning
**Characteristics**: Project completion, transition, reflection

**Leader Actions**:
- Celebrate accomplishments
- Conduct retrospectives
- Document learnings
- Support transitions
- Maintain relationships

## Team Health Assessment

### Assessment Dimensions (1-10 Scale)

1. **Psychological Safety**: Can we take risks without fear?
2. **Dependability**: Do we deliver on commitments?
3. **Clarity**: Are roles and goals clear?
4. **Meaning**: Is work personally important?
5. **Impact**: Does our work matter?
6. **Collaboration**: Do we work well together?
7. **Innovation**: Do we generate new ideas?
8. **Execution**: Do we deliver results?
9. **Engagement**: Are we motivated and energized?
10. **Development**: Are we growing our skills?

### Assessment Methods
- Monthly team health surveys
- Quarterly retrospectives
- 1-on-1 conversations
- Observation in meetings
- Performance metrics
- 360 feedback

## Team Rituals & Practices

### Daily
- **Standup** (15 min): Synchronize, surface blockers
- **Pair/Mob Programming**: Collaborate on complex work

### Weekly
- **Team Sync** (1 hour): Align on priorities and progress
- **Demo**: Show work to stakeholders
- **1-on-1s**: Individual coaching and support

### Bi-Weekly/Sprint
- **Sprint Planning**: Commit to goals
- **Sprint Review**: Demo and gather feedback
- **Retrospective**: Reflect and improve

### Monthly
- **Team Health Check**: Assess and address issues
- **Learning Session**: Share knowledge
- **Team Building**: Build connection

### Quarterly
- **OKR Planning**: Set goals
- **Team Offsite**: Strategic planning and bonding
- **Skills Assessment**: Identify development needs

## High-Performance Team Characteristics

### Results-Focused
- Clear goals and metrics
- Outcome over output mindset
- Data-driven decisions
- Continuous improvement
- Accountability for results

### Collaborative
- Shared ownership and success
- Help each other proactively
- Cross-functional effectiveness
- Open information sharing
- Constructive conflict

### Innovative
- Psychological safety to experiment
- Encourage new ideas
- Fail fast and learn
- Challenge status quo
- Continuous learning

### Customer-Obsessed
- Start with customer needs
- Regular customer interaction
- Act on customer feedback
- Measure customer satisfaction
- Customer stories shared regularly

### Autonomous
- Self-organizing and self-managing
- Make decisions without micromanagement
- Own outcomes end-to-end
- Trusted by leadership
- Clear boundaries and constraints

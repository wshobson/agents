# Leadership Principles мировых компаний

## Amazon Leadership Principles

### 1. Customer Obsession
Leaders start with the customer and work backwards. They work vigorously to earn and keep customer trust.

### 2. Ownership
Leaders are owners. They think long term and don't sacrifice long-term value for short-term results.

### 3. Invent and Simplify
Leaders expect and require innovation and invention from their teams and always find ways to simplify.

### 4. Are Right, A Lot
Leaders are right a lot. They have strong judgment and good instincts. They seek diverse perspectives.

### 5. Learn and Be Curious
Leaders are never done learning and always seek to improve themselves. They are curious about new possibilities.

### 6. Hire and Develop the Best
Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire and promotion. They recognize exceptional talent.

### 7. Insist on the Highest Standards
Leaders have relentlessly high standards. They continually raise the bar and drive their teams to deliver high quality products.

### 8. Think Big
Thinking small is a self-fulfilling prophecy. Leaders create and communicate a bold direction that inspires results.

### 9. Bias for Action
Speed matters in business. Many decisions and actions are reversible and do not need extensive study.

### 10. Frugality
Accomplish more with less. Constraints breed resourcefulness, self-sufficiency, and invention.

### 11. Earn Trust
Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, and treat others respectfully. They are vocally self-critical.

### 12. Dive Deep
Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to the details, audit frequently, and are skeptical when metrics differ from anecdote.

### 13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
Leaders are obligated to respectfully challenge decisions when they disagree, even when doing so is uncomfortable.

### 14. Deliver Results
Leaders focus on the key inputs for their business and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely fashion.

### 15. Strive to be Earth's Best Employer
Leaders work every day to create a safer, more productive, higher performing work environment.

### 16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility
We started in a garage, but we're not there anymore. We must be humble and thoughtful about our role in the world.

## Netflix Culture Principles

### 1. Freedom and Responsibility
We give people more freedom, and expect more responsibility.

### 2. Context, Not Control
Highly aligned, loosely coupled. Set context, not detailed orders.

### 3. People Over Process
The best process is to have great people working together effectively.

### 4. Candor
Say what you think, even if it's controversial, in order to help the company.

### 5. Innovation
Encourage experimentation, even if short-term mistakes.

### 6. Selflessness
You make time to help colleagues. You share information openly and proactively.

### 7. Courage
You say what you think, even if it's unpopular. You make tough decisions without agonizing.

### 8. High Performance
We're a team, not a family. Keep only highly effective people.

## Google's Culture Elements

### 1. Focus on the User
The user is never wrong. Start with user needs and work backwards.

### 2. Think 10x, Not 10%
Seek solutions that are 10x better, not just incremental improvements.

### 3. Launch and Iterate
Get things launched and iterate rapidly based on data.

### 4. Bet on Technical Insights
Bet on breakthrough technology innovations, not just business innovations.

### 5. Ship and Iterate
Perfect is the enemy of good. Ship early and iterate based on feedback.

### 6. Give Employees 20% Time
Allow engineers to spend 20% time on passion projects (Gmail, AdSense originated this way).

### 7. Hire the Best
Hiring is the most important thing you do. Never compromise on quality.

### 8. Data-Driven Decisions
Use data to make decisions, not gut instinct.

## Применение Leadership Principles

### В найме
- Оценивайте кандидатов по principles
- Задавайте behavioral questions
- Ищите evidence прошлого behavior

**Example question (Amazon "Ownership")**:
"Tell me about a time when you took on something outside your area of responsibility. Why did you do it? What was the outcome?"

### В performance reviews
- Оценка по principles, не только results
- 360-degree feedback based on principles
- Reward demonstration of principles

### В decision-making
- Используйте principles как decision framework
- При сложном решении: "Which principle applies here?"
- При конфликте: "Which principle has priority?"

### В культуре
- Reinforce через recognition
- Celebrate examples of principles in action
- Address violations publicly (when appropriate)

## Создание своих Leadership Principles

### Шаги создания:

1. **Assess Current Culture**
   - What behaviors do we reward today?
   - What behaviors drive success?
   - What behaviors are problematic?

2. **Define Desired Culture**
   - What values matter most to us?
   - What behaviors will drive strategy execution?
   - What makes us unique?

3. **Draft Principles**
   - 10-16 principles (not too many)
   - Clear, specific, actionable
   - Memorable and authentic

4. **Test with Team**
   - Do they resonate?
   - Are they believable?
   - Can we demonstrate them?

5. **Embed in Systems**
   - Hiring criteria
   - Performance evaluation
   - Promotion decisions
   - Recognition programs

6. **Model from Top**
   - Leaders must live the principles
   - Call out when not followed
   - Celebrate when exemplified

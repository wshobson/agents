# Leadership Development Plan Command

Create tailored leadership development plans using the 70-20-10 model and proven practices from world-class organizations.

## Assessment & Goal Setting

### Step 1: Understand Current State

**Individual Profile**:
- Current role and level
- Years of experience
- Previous roles and background
- Career aspirations (2-5 years)
- Strengths and development areas

**Leadership Competencies Assessment** (1-10 scale):

**Strategic Leadership**:
- [ ] Strategic thinking and planning
- [ ] Business acumen and financial fluency
- [ ] Innovation and creativity
- [ ] Decision-making quality

**Execution Excellence**:
- [ ] Results orientation and accountability
- [ ] Problem-solving and analytical thinking
- [ ] Operational excellence
- [ ] Priority management

**People Leadership**:
- [ ] Team building and development
- [ ] Coaching and mentoring
- [ ] Communication and influence
- [ ] Conflict resolution
- [ ] Emotional intelligence

**Personal Leadership**:
- [ ] Self-awareness and reflection
- [ ] Learning agility
- [ ] Resilience and adaptability
- [ ] Integrity and ethics
- [ ] Executive presence

### Step 2: Define Development Goals

**Career Aspiration**:
- Target role or level (e.g., VP, Senior Director)
- Timeline (1-2 years, 3-5 years)
- Key capabilities needed for target role
- Gap between current and target

**Development Focus Areas** (Select 2-4):
1. [Priority competency 1]
2. [Priority competency 2]
3. [Priority competency 3]

**Success Criteria**:
- What will you be able to do?
- What results will demonstrate growth?
- What feedback will you receive?
- What level of impact will you have?

## 70-20-10 Development Plan

### 70% - Learning from Experience

**Stretch Assignments** (3-6 months):
- Lead cross-functional initiative
- Own P&L or business unit
- Turn around struggling team/project
- Launch new product or market
- Manage through crisis or change
- Scale team or organization

**Job Rotations** (6-12 months):
- Lateral move to new function
- Geographic relocation
- Customer-facing role
- Staff role (strategy, finance, HR)

**Project Leadership**:
- Strategic planning projects
- M&A integration
- Digital transformation
- Process improvement initiative

**Examples by Development Area**:

| Development Area | Experience Opportunities |
|------------------|-------------------------|
| Strategic Thinking | Lead annual planning, M&A analysis, new market entry |
| Change Leadership | Lead org transformation, system implementation |
| P&L Management | Own business unit, product line, or geography |
| Team Building | Build team from scratch, integrate acquired teams |
| Stakeholder Mgmt | Lead board presentation, partner negotiation |
| Innovation | Lead innovation lab, new product incubation |

### 20% - Learning from Others

**Coaching** (Ongoing):
- Executive coach (external)
- Leadership coach (internal)
- Meet bi-weekly or monthly
- Focus on specific development areas

**Mentoring** (6-12 months):
- Senior executive mentor
- Cross-functional leader mentor
- Industry expert outside company
- Meet monthly for guidance

**Feedback & 360s**:
- Quarterly 360-degree feedback
- Monthly feedback from manager
- Peer feedback on specific projects
- Direct report feedback

**Networking**:
- Join executive peer groups
- Industry associations and forums
- CEO/executive roundtables
- Board advisor opportunities

**Shadowing**:
- Shadow CEO in board meetings
- Shadow other executives
- Observe executive team dynamics
- Learn decision-making processes

### 10% - Formal Learning

**Executive Education**:
- Harvard/Stanford/Wharton executive programs
- Leadership development programs
- Industry-specific executive education
- Board governance courses

**Targeted Training**:
- Strategic thinking workshops
- Executive presence coaching
- Negotiation and influence
- Financial acumen for leaders
- Change management certification

**Self-Directed Learning**:
- Books and articles (monthly)
- Podcasts and videos
- Online courses (Coursera, LinkedIn Learning)
- TED talks and conferences

**Recommended Reading by Area**:

**Strategic Leadership**:
- "Good to Great" - Jim Collins
- "The Innovator's Dilemma" - Clayton Christensen
- "Playing to Win" - Roger Martin

**Execution**:
- "High Output Management" - Andy Grove
- "Measure What Matters" - John Doerr
- "The 4 Disciplines of Execution"

**People Leadership**:
- "Radical Candor" - Kim Scott
- "The Culture Code" - Daniel Coyle
- "Multipliers" - Liz Wiseman

**Personal**:
- "Thinking, Fast and Slow" - Daniel Kahneman
- "Emotional Intelligence 2.0" - Travis Bradberry
- "Dare to Lead" - Brené Brown

## Development by Leadership Level

### First-Time Manager → Senior Manager

**Key Transitions**:
- Individual contributor → people manager
- Tactical → strategic thinking
- Personal results → team results
- Direct work → work through others

**Focus Areas**:
- Delegation and empowerment
- Giving effective feedback
- Performance management
- Building team culture
- Time management and priorities

**70% Experiences**:
- Manage team of 5-8 people
- Lead cross-functional project
- Own feature or product area
- Hire and onboard team members

**20% Relationships**:
- First-time manager coach
- Peer manager mentor
- Monthly skip-level with director

**10% Learning**:
- First-time manager training
- Radical Candor, High Output Management
- Crucial Conversations workshop

### Senior Manager → Director

**Key Transitions**:
- Managing ICs → managing managers
- Execution → strategy
- Single team → multiple teams
- Direct oversight → delegation

**Focus Areas**:
- Strategic planning
- Managing through others
- Cross-functional influence
- Resource allocation
- Organizational design

**70% Experiences**:
- Manage managers (2-4 direct reports)
- Lead department or function
- Own significant business outcomes
- Drive organizational change

**20% Relationships**:
- Executive coach
- VP-level mentor
- Peer director cohort

**10% Learning**:
- Executive education (strategy, finance)
- Strategic thinking workshops
- Books on organizational leadership

### Director → VP/Executive

**Key Transitions**:
- Functional → enterprise thinking
- Execution → vision and strategy
- Stakeholder → executive presence
- Operational → board-level

**Focus Areas**:
- Vision and strategy development
- Executive presence and communication
- Board and stakeholder management
- Business acumen and P&L
- Cultural and organizational leadership

**70% Experiences**:
- Own P&L or major business unit
- Lead strategic initiatives (M&A, transformation)
- Present to board
- Represent company externally

**20% Relationships**:
- CEO/C-suite sponsor
- External CEO mentor
- Board advisor opportunities

**10% Learning**:
- Executive programs (Harvard, Stanford)
- Board governance training
- Books on executive leadership

## Tracking Progress

### Monthly Check-Ins

**Agenda** (30-45 min with manager):
1. Progress on development goals
2. Learnings and insights
3. Challenges or blockers
4. Feedback received
5. Adjustments needed

### Quarterly Reviews

**Comprehensive Assessment**:
- Progress toward each goal (%)
- Competency ratings (current vs target)
- 360 feedback results
- Project outcomes and impact
- Adjust plan based on learning

### Annual Reassessment

**Full Review**:
- Achievement of annual goals
- Updated competency assessment
- New career aspirations
- Next year's development plan
- Promotion or new role readiness

## Output Deliverables

Provide:
1. **Complete development plan** document
2. **70-20-10 activity breakdown** with timelines
3. **Success metrics dashboard** for tracking
4. **Resource list** (coaches, programs, books)
5. **Review calendar** with scheduled check-ins
6. **Manager support brief** on how to help

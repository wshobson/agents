# Полное руководство по фандрайзингу

## Стадии фандрайзинга

### Pre-Seed ($100K - $500K)
**Timing**: Идея → early MVP
**Investors**: Angels, friends & family, micro VCs
**Valuation**: $1M - $5M post-money
**Dilution**: 10-20%

**Что нужно показать**:
- Strong founding team
- Clear problem & solution
- Initial customer validation
- Technical feasibility

### Seed ($500K - $3M)
**Timing**: MVP → early traction
**Investors**: Seed funds, angels, early-stage VCs
**Valuation**: $5M - $15M post-money
**Dilution**: 15-25%

**Что нужно показать**:
- Product in market
- Initial user traction (1K-10K users)
- Evidence of product-market fit
- Clear unit economics path
- Repeatable acquisition channel

### Series A ($5M - $15M)
**Timing**: Product-market fit → scale
**Investors**: Top-tier VCs (Sequoia, a16z, Benchmark)
**Valuation**: $20M - $50M post-money
**Dilution**: 20-30%

**Что нужно показать**:
- Strong product-market fit
- $1M+ ARR with 100%+ YoY growth
- Retention >80% (monthly) or >90% (annual)
- LTV:CAC > 3:1
- Proven go-to-market playbook
- Path to $100M ARR

### Series B ($20M - $50M)
**Timing**: Scaling go-to-market
**Investors**: Growth VCs, crossover funds
**Valuation**: $75M - $200M post-money
**Dilution**: 15-25%

**Что нужно показать**:
- $10M+ ARR with 100%+ YoY growth
- Multiple proven GTM channels
- Net revenue retention >120%
- Clear path to market leadership
- International expansion or product expansion

### Series C+ ($50M - $150M+)
**Timing**: Market dominance
**Investors**: Late-stage VCs, growth equity
**Valuation**: $300M - $1B+ post-money
**Dilution**: 10-20%

**Что нужно показать**:
- $50M+ ARR, 50%+ growth
- Market leadership position
- Rule of 40 trending positive
- Path to profitability clear
- IPO or strategic exit in 2-3 years

## Key Metrics по стадиям

### Seed Stage
- Weekly Active Users (WAU) growth >10% WoW
- D1, D7, D30 retention >40% / >20% / >10%
- Time to value <1 hour
- Qualitative feedback intensity (NPS >30)

### Series A
- Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR): $100K+
- MoM growth: >15%
- CAC payback: <12 months
- LTV:CAC: >3:1
- Net Revenue Retention: >100%
- Magic Number: >0.75

### Series B+
- Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR): $10M+
- YoY growth: >100% (A), >80% (B)
- Gross margin: >70%
- Rule of 40: Growth% + Profit% = 40+
- NRR: >120%
- Sales efficiency: <$1.00 spent per $1 ARR

## Fundraising Timeline

**6 месяцев до раунда**:
- Build investor relationships
- Share monthly updates
- Attend VC events
- Get warm intros

**3 месяца до**:
- Finalize pitch deck
- Build financial model
- Prepare data room
- Target investor list (20-30 firms)

**Month 1-2: Meetings**:
- Week 1-2: Initial meetings (15-20 VCs)
- Week 3-4: Partner meetings (10 firms)
- Week 5-6: Deep dives & diligence (5-7 firms)

**Month 3: Close**:
- Week 7: Term sheets (2-3 offers)
- Week 8-9: Negotiate terms
- Week 10-12: Legal docs & close

**Total timeline**: 2-3 months (если prepared)

## Investor Outreach Strategy

### Warm Introduction Path
1. **Best**: Founder in portfolio → Partner
2. **Good**: Angel investor → Partner
3. **OK**: Lawyer, banker, recruiter → Partner
4. **Last resort**: Cold email (1% response rate)

### Email Template
```
Subject: [Intro: You → Founder @ Company] (from [Mutual Contact])

Hi [Partner Name],

[Mutual Contact] suggested I reach out.

We're [Company] - [one-line description]. We've grown from $0 to $2M ARR in 18 months, entirely through word-of-mouth.

We're raising a $10M Series A to scale our go-to-market and we'd love your feedback.

Deck attached. Would you be open to a 30min call next week?

Best,
[Founder]

P.S. Recent milestone: [impressive metric]
```

## Negotiating Term Sheets

### Key Terms to Negotiate

**Valuation**:
- Pre-money valuation
- Fully diluted capitalization
- Option pool (pre or post-money?)

**Liquidation Preference**:
- 1x non-participating (standard)
- ❌ Avoid: >1x or participating

**Board Composition**:
- Typical: 2 founders, 2 investors, 1 independent
- Keep founder control early

**Pro-rata Rights**:
- Gives investors right to maintain %
- Standard and fair

**Anti-dilution**:
- Broad-based weighted average (standard)
- ❌ Avoid: Full ratchet

**Drag-Along Rights**:
- Standard, allows majority to force sale
- Reasonable protection

**Redemption Rights**:
- ❌ Avoid if possible
- Standard: 5+ years

**No-shop Clause**:
- 30-45 days (not 60+)
- Start date = when diligence begins

### Red Flags in Term Sheets

- ❌ >2x liquidation preference
- ❌ Participating preferred
- ❌ Full ratchet anti-dilution
- ❌ Redemption rights <5 years
- ❌ Excessive board control
- ❌ Pay-to-play provisions
- ❌ Super pro-rata rights

## Due Diligence Checklist

### Business Diligence
- [ ] Financial statements (3 years)
- [ ] Cap table and shareholder agreements
- [ ] Customer contracts (top 10)
- [ ] Revenue breakdown by customer
- [ ] Sales pipeline and forecast
- [ ] Unit economics calculations
- [ ] Cohort analysis

### Product & Technical
- [ ] Product roadmap
- [ ] Technical architecture docs
- [ ] Security & compliance certifications
- [ ] IP ownership documentation
- [ ] Key technical risks

### Legal
- [ ] Certificate of incorporation
- [ ] Bylaws and shareholder agreements
- [ ] Employee agreements and option grants
- [ ] IP assignments
- [ ] Material contracts
- [ ] Litigation history

### Team
- [ ] Organization chart
- [ ] Employee census
- [ ] Key person dependencies
- [ ] Compensation benchmarks
- [ ] Unvested equity

## Common Mistakes

1. ❌ Raising too early (before traction)
2. ❌ Optimizing for valuation over partner fit
3. ❌ Not building relationships before fundraise
4. ❌ Pitching to wrong stage investors
5. ❌ Weak financials / unit economics
6. ❌ No urgency / FOMO
7. ❌ Poor storytelling
8. ❌ Overly defensive about weaknesses
9. ❌ Not knowing your numbers cold
10. ❌ Taking money from wrong investors

## OKR Examples by Product Area

### Growth OKRs

**Objective**: Accelerate user acquisition and activation

**Key Results**:
- Increase DAU from 50K to 100K
- Improve 7-day retention from 35% to 50%
- Achieve viral coefficient of 1.2 (up from 0.8)

---

### Engagement OKRs

**Objective**: Drive deeper product engagement

**Key Results**:
- Increase WAU/MAU ratio from 50% to 65%
- Increase avg sessions per user from 12 to 20/week
- Achieve 70% adoption of core feature (up from 45%)

---

### Monetization OKRs

**Objective**: Build sustainable revenue model

**Key Results**:
- Launch paid tier with 15% free-to-paid conversion
- Increase ARPU from $12 to $18/month
- Reach $100K MRR by quarter end

---

### Platform OKRs

**Objective**: Build scalable, reliable infrastructure

**Key Results**:
- Reduce p99 latency from 2s to 500ms
- Achieve 99.9% uptime (from 98.5%)
- Support 10x traffic growth with zero downtime

## Tracking & Review

### Weekly Check-In
- Progress update (% complete)
- Blockers and risks
- Help needed

### Monthly Review
- Grade progress (on track / at risk / off track)
- Adjust tactics as needed
- Communicate to stakeholders

### Quarter-End Scoring
- Score each KR (0.0 - 1.0)
- Document learnings
- Plan next quarter

**Scoring Guide**:
- 0.7+ = Success (ambitious goals)
- 0.4-0.7 = Progress made
- <0.4 = Missed (analyze why)

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- ❌ Too many OKRs (>3 objectives)
- ❌ KRs that are tasks, not outcomes
- ❌ Unambitious goals (easily achieved)
- ❌ KRs without clear ownership
- ❌ No connection to company strategy

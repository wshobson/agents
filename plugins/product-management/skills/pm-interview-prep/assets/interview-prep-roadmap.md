# 6-Week Interview Preparation Roadmap

Complete week-by-week guide to prepare for CPO, Head of Product, and Product Manager interviews at Google, AWS, Microsoft, OpenAI, and Anthropic.

---

## Overview

**Timeline**: 6 weeks (adjustable based on your schedule)
**Commitment**: 15-20 hours per week
**Goal**: Be fully prepared for product leadership interviews at top tech companies

**Week-by-Week Breakdown:**
- **Week 1-2**: Learn frameworks, write STAR stories, company research
- **Week 3-4**: Practice questions, mock interviews, technical prep
- **Week 5**: Company-specific deep dive, full mock loops
- **Week 6**: Final polish, rest, confidence building

---

## Week 2: Practice & Stories

**Goal**: Practice questions and complete all 20 STAR stories

**Total Time**: 20 hours

### Day 8: Product Design Practice (4 hours)

**All Day: Do 6 Product Design Questions (40 min each)**

Use CIRCLES for every question:

1. "Design a product for blind users"
2. "Improve Spotify for podcast listeners"
3. "Design a fitness app for busy professionals"
4. "Create a product to reduce food waste"
5. "Design a product for remote team collaboration"
6. "Improve LinkedIn for job seekers"

**Practice Process**:
- Set timer for 40 minutes
- Use CIRCLES framework
- Write or record your answer
- Review: What went well? What to improve?

**What to work on**:
- Speed (finish in 35-40 min)
- Structure (clear signposting: "First...", "Second...")
- Depth (detailed solutions, not superficial)

---

### Day 9: Metrics Practice (4 hours)

**All Day: Do 6 Metrics Questions (40 min each)**

Use GAME for every question:

1. "How would you measure success for Instagram Reels?"
2. "Netflix watch time is down 5%. Root cause?"
3. "What metrics matter for AWS Lambda?"
4. "Uber Eats GMV is down 10%. Diagnose."
5. "How would you measure success for ChatGPT?"
6. "Slack's DAU is up but revenue is flat. Why?"

**Practice Process**:
- Set timer for 40 minutes
- Use GAME framework
- Cover North Star, AARRR, counter-metrics
- Explain A/B testing approach

**What to work on**:
- Metric selection (choosing North Star)
- Funnel thinking (Acquisition ‚Üí Retention ‚Üí Revenue)
- Diagnostic approach (segment, compare, hypothesize)

### Day 10: Technical Practice (4 hours)

**Morning (2 hours): Learn System Design Basics**

- [ ] Read system design framework guide (1 hour)
- [ ] Watch system design interview video (1 hour)

**Resources**:
- "System Design Interview" book by Alex Xu
- YouTube: "System Design Interview" channel
- Exponent: Technical PM interview videos

**Afternoon (2 hours): Practice 2 System Design Questions**

1. "Design Twitter's backend" (1 hour)
2. "Design Uber's dispatch system" (1 hour)

**What to cover**:
- Requirements (functional + non-functional)
- Estimation (users, storage, bandwidth)
- High-level architecture (components, data flow)
- Trade-offs (CAP theorem, consistency vs availability)

**Note**: PMs don't need to know all implementation details, but should understand architecture, trade-offs, and how to discuss with engineers.

**Template** (from Amazon):
```
[Press Release - 200 words]
- Headline
- Subhead
- Problem
- Solution
- Executive quote
- Customer quote
- Call to action

[FAQ - 500 words]
```

## Final Tips

**1. Trust Your Preparation**

You've done 60-100 hours of prep. You're ready. More studying at this point has diminishing returns.

**2. Be Yourself**

Interviewers evaluate culture fit, not just skills. Show your authentic self, passion, and personality.

**3. It's a Two-Way Street**

You're also evaluating if the company is right for YOU. Ask questions, gather information, trust your gut.

**4. Frameworks Are Guides, Not Scripts**

Use frameworks to structure your thinking, but adapt based on the question and conversation. Don't sound robotic.

**5. Quantify Everything**

Numbers make stories memorable and believable. "Increased engagement" is weak. "Increased DAU from 1M to 1.5M (+50%)" is strong.

**6. Show Your Learning**

Every STAR story should end with what you learned. Growth mindset is universally valued.

**7. Energy Matters**

Enthusiasm and passion are contagious. Smile, show excitement, engage with the interviewer.

**8. Silence Is Okay**

It's okay to pause and think. "Let me think about that for a moment" shows thoughtfulness, not weakness.

**9. Ask for Clarification**

If a question is unclear, ask! "Just to clarify, are you asking about [X] or [Y]?" shows careful thinking.

**10. You've Got This!**

You're well-prepared. You have great stories. You're a strong PM. Now go show them! üöÄ
